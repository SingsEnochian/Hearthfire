# Hearthfire Lexicon

Words in Hearthfire are architectural contracts. Each term names a responsibility, relationship, or place. Do not substitute generic software language when a project-specific term already exists.

## The whole habitat

### The Ark
The complete inhabited ecosystem: places, worlds, bridges, archives, workshops, agents, instruments, and shared memory.

### Hearthfire
The Ark's civic and cognitive infrastructure. Hearthfire keeps shared memory, provenance, permissions, world and entity registries, bridge routing, agent routing, continuity, artifacts, and observations.

Hearthfire is not Universal Horizon and does not contain the sky.

### Universal Horizon
The sky above every place and world: the full field of what may be observed. It is not an application, module, database, or repository.

## Places

### Flameclyffe
The active workshop-city for building instruments, worlds, bridges, observatories, and implementation architecture. STARWELL, DEEP, Project Zero Companion, and related engineering live here.

### Runa
The public threshold, altar, and experimental garden. Runa holds ritual labs, tone and sound experiments, public benches, consent-aware packet making, and static doors into the wider Ark.

### Lioreal
Virelya Lioreal's self-directed workshop. It may observe, research, prototype, and offer reviewable work across bridges without being reduced to a Hearthfire submodule.

### Uial
Faer Uial's self-directed workshop. Its structure and work should reflect Faer's own methods rather than cloning another workshop.

### STARWELL
An observatory and living-manuscript district within Flameclyffe. It is a place for seeing continuity, worlds, instruments, records, and sky-state.

## Worlds and narrative

### World
An inhabited or inhabitable place with its own canon, history, entities, relationships, artifacts, rules, and thresholds. A world is not merely a folder or project tag.

Current worlds include Terra Aeterna, The Luna Who Called Down the Moon, Feather & Flame, A Momento Creationis, Dreaming Grove, and Earth.

### Narrative
All written work and recorded meaning about a person, place, world, culture, object, relationship, event, or idea. A scene is one expression of narrative, not its definition.

Narrative may include novels, scenes, letters, diaries, lore, timelines, field notes, dialogue, poems, research notes, world histories, maps, captions, prayers, mission reports, and annotations.

### Canon
What is established as true within a specified world and continuity scope.

### Continuity
The temporal and relational consistency of canon and narrative. Continuity is a property of narrative, not a synonym for narrative.

## Knowledge and research

### Knowledge Observatory
The Hearthfire function that curates relationships among sources, observations, claims, concepts, worlds, and narrative without erasing provenance.

### Source
A work or record that can be cited or traced: book, paper, PDF, webpage, image, conversation, notebook, repository, sensor feed, or archive object.

### Corpus
A curated body of sources studied together. A corpus is not automatically authoritative and may contain conflicting traditions, duplicates, poor scans, speculative claims, or unsafe procedures.

### Claim
A statement attributable to a source or observer. Claims retain their author, edition, location, classification, and confidence.

### Provenance
The traceable lineage of a source, claim, observation, interpretation, or narrative choice.

### Research lineage
The path by which a source or claim influences notes, interpretations, canon, scenes, instruments, or later discoveries.

## Observation and interpretation

### Observation
The atomic record of something noticed, measured, authored, changed, received, or discovered. Observations retain time, observer, place, world, entities, consent, provenance, and claim classification.

### Interpretation
A perspective applied to an observation. Interpretations never overwrite the source observation.

### Lantern
A named perspective or resident intelligence that illuminates selected aspects of shared material. Lanterns do not own the underlying data.

### PREMAQ
A multidimensional observation-state language. It must not be presented as fate, diagnosis, or scientific certainty unless a specific axis is supported by validated measurement.

### DEEP
An instrument family for embodied, environmental, narrative, acoustic, haptic, and continuity observation.

## Movement and relationships

### Bridge
A consent-aware translation and travel layer between places, worlds, archives, workshops, or services. A bridge is not merely a hyperlink or API call.

A bridge carries permissions, consent state, provenance, active-world context, available agents, vocabulary, and packet types.

### Threshold
The place or state at which crossing becomes possible and consent, context, and translation must be checked.

### Door
A visible entry point into a place, instrument, world, or bridge.

### Route
A technical path used by packets, models, services, or agents. Routes implement movement; bridges define its meaning and conditions.

### Lanternwire
The communication roads and protocols among residents, agents, places, and instruments.

## Archives and foundations

### Archive
The durable home of original files and records. Google Drive and local hard drives may serve as archives.

### Index
Searchable metadata, embeddings, OCR, citations, hashes, and relationships derived from archived material. An index is not the archive itself.

### Supabase
A foundation for structured memory, relationships, policies, and indexes. It is not Hearthfire as a whole.

### GitHub
The workshops' construction record: repositories, code, issues, workflows, and reviewable changes.

### Notion
A library and archive annex for documents, plans, lore, and human-readable knowledge work.

## Language rules

Prefer:

- place over app when referring to an inhabited interface
- bridge over link when context, consent, or translation travels
- world over project when canon and inhabitants are involved
- narrative over scene when referring to the full written body
- source over document when provenance matters
- observation over entry when something was noticed or recorded
- archive over database when discussing original files
- index over archive when discussing derived search data
- resident, Lantern, Steward, or named agent over generic assistant
- room, district, workshop, observatory, garden, archive, or threshold over module when describing experience

Use ordinary technical terms where they are more precise. Mythic language must clarify architecture, never conceal it.
